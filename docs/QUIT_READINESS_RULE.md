# Quit Readiness Rule (Household-Aware, One Page)

Use this for a monthly decision check with your wife before leaving full-time employment.

## Honest Starting Point (March 2026)

- Combined household net income: `$4,000/month` (both jobs, ~50/50 split)
- Monthly expenses: ~`$4,000/month` (currently paycheck to paycheck — no surplus)
- Current savings buffer: `$0` (no emergency runway yet)
- Solo-dev income today: `$0` (Sticker Quest is pre-revenue)

This is the real baseline. The plan below is where we are going, not where we are now.

## Core Principle

Do not make a quit decision based on one strong month. Make the decision on consistency, runway, and downside protection. And never make it while still paycheck to paycheck.

## Step Zero (Before Quit Gates Even Apply)

These must be true before the quit scorecard is relevant:

- Build and hold a starter emergency buffer of at least `$1,000` first
- Monthly expenses are covered with some surplus left over
- Solo-dev income has crossed `$500/month` consistently for at least 3 months
- Savings balance is growing, not holding steady or shrinking

Until Step Zero is complete, the goal is simple: ship Sticker Quest and prove the revenue model works at any scale.

## Two Runway Numbers (Use Both)

1. **Gap runway (household-aware):**
   - Formula: `12 x (monthly expenses - spouse reliable income)`
   - Estimated with current numbers: `12 x ($4,000 - $2,000) = $24,000`
   - Meaning: minimum cash buffer to cover Ryan's side for 12 months if he leaves his job.

2. **Full-burn runway (contingency):**
   - Formula: `12 x monthly expenses`
   - Estimated with current numbers: `12 x $4,000 = $48,000`
   - Meaning: protects the family if spouse income drops or costs increase.

Note: these numbers will need to be recalculated once actual monthly expenses are tracked separately from income.

## Recommended Quit Gate (Safer Version)

Only quit when all are true:

- Step Zero is complete (surplus exists, savings growing)
- Runway is at least `12 months` by the gap-runway model (`$24,000+` at current split)
- Preferred safety reserve is in progress toward full-burn protection (`$48,000` target)
- Solo-dev net income is at least `$5,000/month` for `6 consecutive months`
- No single customer/channel is over `60%` of new revenue
- One bad month stress test still works on paper without immediate panic cuts

## Panic Line (Hard Stop Rule)

If either happens, immediately switch to protection mode:

- **Cash panic line:** savings are below `$1,000` at any point before quitting, or below `8 months` of gap-runway coverage after quitting, or
- **Income panic line:** solo-dev income drops below Ryan's income share (`$2,000`) for `2 consecutive months` after quitting

## Protection Mode (When Panic Line Triggers)

- Freeze all new scope for 30 days
- Focus work on revenue reliability first (retention, conversion, repeat sales)
- Cut optional spending until runway stabilizes
- Re-run monthly forecast with conservative assumptions only

## Monthly Decision Scorecard (10-Minute Review)

Track these each month:

- Current savings balance
- Runway months (gap model and full-burn model)
- Last 6 months of solo-dev net income
- Revenue concentration (% from top channel)
- Stress-test result: "Could we handle one weak month right now?"

Status:

- **Green:** safe to continue toward quit window
- **Yellow:** hold, stabilize for another month
- **Red:** do not quit, enter protection mode now

## Plain-English Family Rule

Quitting is allowed only when it is safe for both ambition and family stability.
If numbers conflict, family stability wins.
