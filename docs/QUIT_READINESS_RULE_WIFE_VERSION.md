# Our Family Plan: When Is It Safe for Ryan to Go Solo Full-Time?

This is our shared agreement. We review it together once a month.

---

## Where We Are Right Now (March 2026)

- We bring home about **$4,000/month** combined, roughly half each
- Our expenses are about equal to what we bring in — we are paycheck to paycheck right now
- We currently have **$0 savings runway**
- Ryan's app (Sticker Quest) is still being built — it makes **$0 right now**

This plan is not "Ryan quits soon." This plan is **what needs to be true before that is even a conversation.**

---

## Step One: Prove the Idea Works at Any Scale

Before talking about quitting, Ryan's app needs to generate consistent income — even small amounts.

The first checkpoint is simple:
- Build our first emergency buffer of at least **$1,000**
- App is generating at least **$500/month** for 3 months in a row
- Our household expenses are covered with something left over
- Our savings balance is growing, not holding steady or shrinking

Until that happens, the focus is on building and shipping — not on quit timing.

---

## The Real Quit Gate (When It Finally Applies)

Ryan only leaves his full-time job when **all** of this is true:

1. We have at least **$24,000 saved** (12 months to cover Ryan's half of expenses if needed)
2. Our goal safety reserve is **$48,000** (12 months of full household costs — protects us if anything changes on my end too)
3. Ryan's solo income is **$5,000+ per month for 6 months in a row**
4. That income is not coming from just one fragile source
5. We could survive one bad month without immediate stress

---

## The Panic Line (Our Hard Stop)

If either of these happens after Ryan has quit, we stop pushing growth and protect the family first:

- Before quitting: our savings are below **$1,000** at any point, or
- After quitting: our savings drop below **8 months of runway**, or
- Ryan's solo income drops below **$2,000/month for 2 months in a row**

When the panic line is hit, for 30 days:
- Freeze new projects and ideas
- Focus only on what brings in reliable income
- Cut optional spending
- Review the numbers together before making any next moves

---

## Our Monthly 10-Minute Check-In

Once a month, we look at:

- How much is saved and how many months does it cover?
- What did Ryan's app make last month? Last 6 months?
- Is income coming from more than one place?
- Does this feel manageable for both of us?

---

## Our Final Rule

**If ambition and family stability ever conflict, family stability wins — every time.**

This plan exists so that when the day comes, we both feel ready — not just Ryan.
